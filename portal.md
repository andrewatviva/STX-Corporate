## What Is the STX Corporate Travel Portal?

The STX Corporate Travel Portal is a **purpose-built travel management platform** designed for organisations that need:
- Centralised oversight of staff travel requests
- Structured approval workflows before travel is confirmed
- Detailed tracking of travel spend across departments and cost centres
- Compliance with travel policy budgets
- Accessible design for all staff regardless of ability

Unlike generic booking tools, the portal is **built around your organisation's structure** — your teams, your approval chains, your cost centres, and your travel policies. STX manages the setup and ongoing support; your team logs in and uses it.

## Who Uses the Portal?

The portal is designed for three types of client users, each with a tailored experience:

### Operations Manager (Client Operations role)
*The person who coordinates travel for the organisation.*
- Submit and manage trip requests on behalf of travellers
- View all travel across the entire account
- Approve trips (by default — can be configured)
- Manage team members and passenger profiles
- Access spend reports and analytics
- Manage team structure and user permissions (if enabled by your STX account manager)

### Approver (Client Approver role)
*The person who authorises travel before it proceeds.*
- Reviews and approves or declines travel requests
- Can be scoped to approve for all staff, specific named staff, or staff in their reporting chain
- Receives email notifications when trips are submitted for approval
- Access to spend reports

### Traveller (Traveller role)
*The person travelling.*
- Submits their own travel requests
- Tracks the status of their trips
- Receives booking confirmations, itinerary notifications, and departure reminders by email
- Rates service providers after travel

> **Note:** Individual permissions are flexible. Operations staff can be granted or denied specific capabilities. A traveller can be given approval rights. A specific user can be restricted to only viewing their own trips. This is managed by STX or, optionally, by your own Operations Manager.

## Core Features

### 1. Travel Dashboard

The dashboard gives an at-a-glance view of your organisation's travel activity.

**What you see:**
- **Status summary cards** — counts of trips at every stage (Draft, Pending Approval, Approved, Booked, Travelling, Completed, Declined, Cancelled) — each card is clickable and navigates to the filtered trip list
- **Spend overview** — total travel spend for the current and previous financial year (Australian FY: July–June), with year-on-year comparison
- **Monthly spend chart** — bar chart comparing current vs previous year month by month
- **Spend by cost centre** — horizontal bar chart showing your top cost centres for the current year
- **Upcoming trips** — approved and booked trips departing in the next 60 days
- **Recent trips** — the 8 most recently updated trips

**Scoped to your role:** Managers see their team's travel. Individuals see their own travel. Operations see the whole account.

---

### 2. Travel Management

The central hub for creating, tracking, and managing all travel requests.

**Creating a trip request:**
1. Click "New Trip" from the Travel page
2. Enter trip details: title, type, traveller, cost centre, dates, origin and destination city
3. Add travel sectors — one or more of: Flight, Accommodation, Car Hire, Parking, Transfers, Meals, Other
4. Each sector captures relevant details (flight number, property name, check-in dates, etc.) and cost
5. Save as a draft, or submit for approval in one click

**Trip types** (configured for your organisation):
- STX-Managed — STX handles all booking on your behalf
- Self-Managed — your team books directly; portal tracks approvals and spend
- Group Event — for multi-traveller group travel

**Trip statuses:**
| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Draft | Saved but not yet submitted |
| Pending Approval | Submitted, waiting for an approver to act |
| Approved | Approved — STX or your team will arrange booking |
| Declined | Declined by approver — can be edited and resubmitted |
| Booked | Travel has been confirmed and arranged |
| Travelling | In progress (automatically derived from dates) |
| Completed | Travel finished |
| Cancelled | Trip cancelled |

**Editing and amendments:**
- Draft and declined trips can be freely edited
- Submitted and approved trips go through an amendment process — each change is logged with a reason and the full history is visible in the trip
- A booking window indicator shows how far in advance trips are being planned (colour-coded from green to red)

**Policy compliance:**
- If your organisation has accommodation or flight spend limits configured, the portal automatically checks each booking against your rates
- Trips that exceed the threshold either show a warning or are automatically sent for approval — depending on your settings

**File attachments:**
- Upload supporting documents to any trip (quotes, approvals, itineraries, receipts)
- Stored securely; accessible to your team and STX

---

### 3. Approval Workflow

**How approvals work:**
1. Traveller or Operations staff submits a trip
2. Designated approvers receive an email notification
3. Approver logs into the portal, reviews the trip, and approves or declines
4. Traveller and trip creator receive email confirmation of the outcome
5. If approved, STX proceeds with booking (for STX-Managed trips)

**Approval flexibility:**
- **Any approver can be scoped** to approve for all staff, specific named individuals, or staff within their reporting hierarchy
- **Hierarchy-based approval:** If an approver manages a team, they can be configured to approve for their direct reports, their direct reports plus one level removed, or two levels removed
- **Multiple approvers:** Multiple people can hold approval rights — the first approval received moves the trip forward
- **Operations can also approve:** Operations Managers can approve trips by default (configurable per user)
- **Per-trip-type approval:** Some trip types can require approval while others (e.g. self-managed) may not — configured at account level

### 4. Passenger Profiles

Store travel profiles for your frequent travellers — ensuring STX has everything needed to book accurately.

**Profile captures:**
- Personal details (name, date of birth, phone, email)
- Emergency contact
- Identity documents (passport, driver's licence, proof of age — configurable)
- Travel loyalty programs (airline frequent flyer, hotel, car rental, rail programs)
- Accessibility requirements — detailed and purpose-built:
  - Disability and support needs
  - Mobility aids (wheelchair details: transfer method, model, dimensions, weight, battery type for power chairs)
  - Carer requirements
  - Dietary requirements
- Travel preferences (seat preference, meal type, accommodation preferences)
- **Profile completeness indicator** — shows as a percentage so you can see at a glance which profiles need updating

**Why this matters:** STX uses passenger profiles to ensure every booking accounts for accessibility needs from the outset — not as an afterthought. Mobility aid details, carer requirements, and dietary needs are captured with the same rigour as flight preferences.

### 5. Team Management

Set up your organisation's structure within the portal.

**What you can manage:**
- Add, edit, and deactivate team member accounts
- Assign roles (Operations, Approver, Traveller)
- Set reporting relationships — who reports to whom
- Assign cost centres to individual users
- Configure approval delegations — who each approver can approve for
- Customise individual user permissions (if enabled for your account)
- View the reporting hierarchy as a tree diagram

**Reporting hierarchy uses:**
- Managers automatically see their direct reports' travel on their dashboard
- Approvers can be scoped to only approve for their reporting chain
- Organisational structure is reflected accurately in spend reports

### 6. Spend Reports & Analytics

Five analytics reports provide full visibility of your organisation's travel spend and patterns.

| Report | What It Shows |
|--------|--------------|
| **All Travel** | Complete filterable trip register — every trip with status, type, cost centre, dates, and booking lead time. Export to CSV. |
| **Average Spend by Destination** | How much travel to each destination costs on average, with per-sector breakdown and per-night accommodation rates |
| **Spend by Departure City** | Where travel originates from — useful for understanding which offices or locations drive travel volume |
| **Hotel Popularity** | Which accommodation properties are most used, with average nightly rates and visit counts |
| **Travel Policy Compliance** | Compares actual accommodation and flight spend against your configured policy rates — shows variance % and $ for each destination |

**All reports include:**
- Date range filters with quick presets (This Month, Last Month, This Quarter, Last Quarter, This Financial Year, Last Financial Year)
- Scoped to your team or the full account based on your role
- Bookings window analysis — are trips being planned far enough in advance?

### 7. Email Notifications

The portal sends automated email notifications at every key stage of the trip lifecycle. Each user can customise which notifications they receive.

| Notification | Sent To | When |
|-------------|---------|------|
| Trip submitted for approval | All eligible approvers | When a trip is submitted |
| Trip approved | Traveller + trip creator | When approved |
| Trip declined | Traveller + trip creator | When declined |
| Trip booked | All travellers on the trip | When marked as booked |
| Digital itinerary ready | All travellers | When STX adds itinerary link |
| Pre-departure reminder | All travellers | 3 days before travel starts |
| Rate your provider | All travellers | 2 days after travel ends |

**Approved and declined notifications are mandatory** — these cannot be turned off as they are critical to the travel workflow.

Users manage their own notification preferences from their account settings — no administrator involvement needed.

## Accessibility Features

> **This is a core differentiator.** The STX Corporate Travel Portal is built to be genuinely usable by everyone on your team — not just those without additional needs. The built-in accessibility toolbar is always available and remembers each user's preferences.

### The Accessibility Toolbar

Available to all users via a floating button at the bottom right of every page. Settings are saved automatically and restored on every visit.

**Text & Reading**
| Feature | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| Text size adjustment | Increase or decrease text size in 10% steps (80% – 160%) — affects all text proportionally |
| Readable font | Switches the entire portal to Lexend, a font designed for improved readability and reduced visual stress |
| Underline links | Adds underlines to all hyperlinks for users who rely on more than colour to identify links |
| Increased line spacing | Adds extra spacing between lines of text — helpful for users with dyslexia or visual processing differences |

**Colour & Contrast**
| Feature | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| High contrast | Increases contrast across the interface for users with low vision |
| Dark contrast | Smart-inverts colours to a dark theme while preserving image colours |
| Light background | Forces a white background on all content areas |
| Grayscale | Removes all colour — useful for colour-blind users or reducing visual distraction |
| Invert colours | Full colour inversion for users who find dark-on-light difficult |

**Navigation**
| Feature | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| Enhanced focus indicators | Adds a prominent 4px blue outline to focused elements — critical for keyboard-only navigation |
| Reduce motion | Disables all animations and transitions — important for users with vestibular disorders or motion sensitivity |

**Eleven features. One toolbar. Zero setup required.**

Each feature can be toggled independently. The combination of active features is shown on the toolbar button as a count badge, and a "Reset all" button appears whenever any customisation is active.

## Flexibility & Customisation

### Account-Level Customisation
Your STX account manager configures the following to match your organisation:

- **Portal title and branding** — your name, logo, and colours
- **Cost centres** — your departments, projects, or funding sources
- **Trip types** — the categories of travel relevant to your organisation
- **Booking components** — the sector types your staff use (flights, accommodation, car hire, transfers, meals, parking, etc.)
- **Approval workflow** — which trip types require approval, and at how many levels
- **Travel policy rates** — accommodation nightly limits and flight cost limits per destination
- **Policy enforcement** — whether exceeding rates shows a warning or triggers mandatory approval
- **Feature set** — which modules are available to your team (reports, hotel booking, file attachments, etc.)
- **GST rate** — Australian GST (10%), NZ GST (15%), or GST-free

### Per-User Customisation
Individual user accounts can be fine-tuned beyond their base role:

- **Permission overrides** — grant or deny specific capabilities to any user (create trips, approve trips, view all team trips, edit passenger profiles, view reports, manage team, etc.)
- **Approval scope** — configure exactly whose travel each approver can authorise
- **Cost centre assignment** — pre-assign users to a cost centre so new trips default correctly
- **Email preferences** — each user manages their own notification subscriptions

### Self-Service Options (Optional)
If enabled for your account, your Operations Manager can:
- Manage team member permissions directly (no need to contact STX for every change)
- Set and update approval delegations as your team structure changes
- Update passenger profiles and team structure at any time

## Data & Security

- **Strict data isolation** — your organisation's data is completely separate from all other clients; enforced at the database security level, not just application logic
- **Role-based access control** — every user sees only what their role permits; security rules are enforced server-side, not just in the interface
- **JWT custom claims** — user roles are encoded in authentication tokens and verified independently on every request
- **Audit trail** — every change to every trip is logged with the user who made it, the timestamp, and exactly what changed
- **Firebase infrastructure** — hosted on Google Firebase (Australian data region); enterprise-grade availability and security
- **Secure file storage** — attachments stored in Firebase Storage with per-tenant access rules; 10 MB per file

## Onboarding Process

Getting your organisation set up on the portal is straightforward:

1. **STX sends you an onboarding link** — a secure, personalised link emailed to your nominated contact
2. **You complete the preferences form** — covers branding, cost centres, trip types, approval workflow, travel policy rates, and more; everything you're unsure about can be left for discussion with STX
3. **STX reviews and configures** — your responses are applied to your portal configuration by your STX account manager
4. **User accounts are created** — STX sets up your team with appropriate roles and temporary passwords
5. **You're live** — log in, review the setup, and start managing travel

The onboarding form does not require any portal login — it's accessible via a secure link that can be completed on any device. Your STX account manager is available throughout to clarify any settings.
